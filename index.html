Rush: A Trubute

Current Members

Alex Lifeson

Alex Lifeson was born August 27th, 1953 in Fernie, British Columbia. Alex founded the band RUSH in 1968 along with John Rutsey and Jeff Jones. Alex's girlfriend gave birth to his eldest son in 1970. Alex then marries his girlfriend in 1975 and she gives birth to their second son in 1977. Alex became an Officer of the Order of Canada on May 9th, 1996. Alex plays the guitars for RUSH. Most his work has been doen with RUSH, though he has worked on several side projects with other bands. To date Alex's sole solo release isVictor

Geddy Lee

Geddy Lee was born in Toronto, Ontario on July 29th, 1953. Geddy and Alex are childhood friends. At the request of Alex, Geddy Lee joins RUSH in September 1968, to replace Jeff Jones. Geddy marries in 1976. Geddy's wife has given birth to a daughter and a son. Geddy Lee became an Officer of the Order of Canada on May 9th, 1996. Geddy Lee Plays Bass and synthesizers fro RUSH while also providing the main vocals. Most of Lee's work has been with RUSH. His pnly solo release is My Favorite Headache, released in 2000.

Neil Peart

Neil Peart was born in Hamilton Ontario on September 12th, 1952. Peart auditions and joins RUSH in 1974 to replace John Rutsey. Peart is the main lyrisist for eveything after the first album. Neil also provides his talents as a drummer to the music of RUSH. Neil is often in lists for top drummers. Neil is also a successful author, publishing several non-fiction, some fiction as well, books. In August of 1997, Neil loses his daughter in a car crash. Only 10 months later his wife dies of cancer. After a hiatus, and meeting his future wife, Neil is ready to come back to RUSH. Neil is very mush introverted compared to his goofy band mates. Neil is now considered retired due to his chronic tendonitis.

Past Members

John Rutsey

John Rutsey was born in Toronto, Ontario on July 23rd, 1952. He joined RUSH in 1968 as drummer. He is the recorded drummer on the first Self-Titled Album. Due to health complications due to his diabetes and musical differences, Rutsey leaves the band and is replaced by Neil Peart. John play's his last show with Rush on July 25th, 1974. John Rutsey died on May 11th, 2008 due to an apparent heart attack, relatd to his diabetes.

Jeff Jones

Jeff Jones was born in Chicago, Illinois on September 10th 1953. He joined RUSH as the Original Vocalist and Bassist in the summer of 1968. His time with RUSH was short Lived, only lasting 3 weeks. Was replaced by Geddy Lee. As of 2015, Jeff Jones is touring with Burton Cummings and his band.

Progressive Rock Gods

Rush is a three-piece rock band from Canada consisting of Geddy Lee(Bass), Alex Lifeson(Guitar), and Neil Peart(Drummer). They released their first album in 1974, which featured John Rutsy on drums as opposed to Neil. They have released 20 studio albums, with their most recent studio release being Clockwork Angels released in 2012. Rush has very successfully gained themselves a dedicated Fan Base who will proclaim their greatness forever. Rush is very progressive when it comes to their sound. Constantly changing, growing, and experimenting, Rush has changed their sound over several albums, but somehow find a way to remain quintessential Rush.

Time Line

  • 1971: The official first incarnation of Rush is formed.
  • 1973: First single is released to lukewarm reception.
  • 1974: The debut of their first album Rush
  • July 25, 1974: Last performance with John Rutsey
  • July 29, 1974: Peart joins on, replacing Rutsey.
  • 1975: The release of Rush's second album Fly By Night
  • 1975: The release of Carress of Steel The album was so unpopular at the time it led to the tour being called the "Down the Tubes Tour"
  • 1976: Despite being pressured to conform to mainstream sounds, Rush Releases 2112 their first commercial success.
  • 1977: Release of A Farewell to Kings Rush continues to delve into prog-rock ideas.
  • 1978: Release of Hemispheres
  • 1980: Release of Permanent Waves Rush's first TOP 5 ALBUM in the US. Which also began a shift away from progressive elements.
  • 1981: Rush enjoy's their peak of popularity with the release of Moving Pictures The perfect Balance of Prog rock and commercial hits.
  • 1982: The release of Signals Which also marked a change in their sound. Beggining to experiment with synthesizer's and playing with genre's such as funk and reggae
  • 1983: Rush and long-time producer Terry Brown part ways due to the new sound.
  • 1984: The release of Grace Under Pressure Saw the continuation of diving deeper into the syntheitc sound. Going as far as Peart using electonic drum sets.
  • 1985: With a new producer, Peter Collins, the release of Power Windows
  • 1987: The release of Hold Your Fire marking the end of the Synth era.
  • 1989: The release of Presto which saw a remarkedly reduced use of keyboards.
  • 1991: The release of Roll the Bones marked a return to three-piece basics.
  • 1993: The release of Counterparts
  • 1996: The release of Test for Echo one of their most guitar-driven sounds.
  • October, 1996: Rush's first tour without an opening act, or "An Evening With Rush"
  • 1997: Neil Peart begins lessons with Freddie Gruber
  • August, 1997: Neil loses his daughter, Selena, to a car accident.
  • June, 1998: Neil loses his wife, Jacqueline, to a battle with cancer
  • 1998: Peart considers himself retired and goes soul searching after his wife's fuineral.
  • September 9, 2000: Peart marries Carrie Nuttal.
  • 2001: Neil announces he is ready to come out of retirement.
  • 2002: The return of Rush and the release of Vapor Trails Marking the first Album since Carress of Steel to use ZERO synthesizers.
  • 2007: The release of Snakes & Arrows, one of the bands Heaviest entries.
  • 2012: The release of Clockwork Angels See the return of conceptualized Albums. And their Last Release.
  • April 18, 2013: Rush's Induction into the Rock and Roll Hall of Fame.
  • May 2015: The start of Rush's last large-scale tour, R40
  • Present: We Rush fans patiently wait a new CD or tour or any info really.
